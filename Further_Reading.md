# Further Reading: Authoritative Ergonomic Resources

Below are high-quality, publicly available guides and articles that provide detailed ergonomic principles and evidence-based recommendations for home office setup.

1. **CDC/NIOSH Computer Workstation Checklist**  
   [https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/media/pdfs/Computer-Workstation-Checklist.pdf](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/media/pdfs/Computer-Workstation-Checklist.pdf)  
   A comprehensive checklist from the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health covering proper posture, chair adjustment, monitor placement, and workstation design to prevent work-related musculoskeletal disorders.

2. **Montana Department of Public Health & Human Services: Home Office Ergonomics**  
   [https://dphhs.mt.gov/assets/employee/Ergonomics.pdf](https://dphhs.mt.gov/assets/employee/Ergonomics.pdf)  
   A practical guide with clear illustrations and tips for monitor height, chair support, foot positioning, and the 20-20-20 rule, specifically tailored for home office environments.

3. **University of Pennsylvania: Ergonomic Considerations in Setting up a Home Office Workspace**  
   [https://ehrs.upenn.edu/sites/default/files/inline-files/Ergonomic%20Considerations%20in%20Setting%20up%20a%20Home%20Office%20Workspace.pdf](https://ehrs.upenn.edu/sites/default/files/inline-files/Ergonomic%20Considerations%20in%20Setting%20up%20a%20Home%20Office%20Workspace.pdf)  
   An academic-style resource that explains neutral positioning, anthropometric variation, and the importance of individualized adjustments to reduce musculoskeletal strain.
